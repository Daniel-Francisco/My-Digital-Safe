FAQ Section:
How does My Digital Safe secure my password?
My Digital Safe used the PBKDF2 with HMAC SHA256. This cryptographic hash function is used to derive the hash to validate your password and ultimately derive the symmetric encryption key to protect your data. Simple version? We take your password and turn it into a irreversible garbled mess of characters using a 'hashing' function. Some very smart mathematicians create and validate the effectiveness of this hashing function and we use it in our application. This 'hash' is impossible to convert back into your password, and we can re-calculate the hash each time you log in. If the hash we calculate matches the stored hash, your login is successful.
How does My Digital Safe secure my data?
We encrypt all of your data using AES-256 algorithm before storing it in a database on your phone. The key to this algorithm is calculated through the PBKDF2 with HMAC SHA256 algorithm during the password hashing process. In simplified terms, we use your password to encrypt and decrypt your data. With a strong password, this data is almost impossible to decrypt. With this in mind, it is important you pick a good password!
